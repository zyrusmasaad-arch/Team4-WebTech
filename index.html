Overview

Misinformation is information that provides people with modified, misleading, or simply false details regarding events, news, and occurrences. While some misinformation might seem harmless, the wrong information can still have drastic consequences depending on the type.

There are many different types of misinformation, phishing, scams, fake news, deepfakes, and more. Knowing the different types helps us develop the right opinions and make informed choices.

But is it really important to learn this? The short answer is "Yes", again mistakes in information can be quite costly especially for those who are easy to believe everything they see online.

This website aims to educate people about misinformation, its types, its effects, and how to prevent falling for it.

Misinformation vs Disinformation

Aspect Misinformation Disinformation
Definition False or inaccurate information shared without harmful intent. Deliberately false information spread with the intent to deceive.
Intent Unintentional — often shared by people who think it’s true. Intentional — creators know it’s false and want to mislead.
Examples Sharing outdated news by mistake. Creating fake news stories, deepfakes, manipulated videos.

Why Care About Misinformation?

Misinformation is not just harmless, it can have serious effects on our society and daily lives. Wrong information leads to wrong opinions, harmful decisions, and loss of trust.

Health Risks

False medical advice can cause people to ignore treatments or reject vaccines, risking lives.

Politics

Fake news can manipulate elections and create division among citizens.

Society

Misinformation creates fear, panic, and sometimes even violence in communities.

Personal Decisions

Believing false information can affect careers, finances, and relationships.

Kinds of Misinformation

Disinformation

Deliberately false or misleading information spread with the intent to deceive. Creating fake news stories, manipulation of images and video.

Malinformation

Information based on facts but deliberately taken out of context to mislead the readers or viewers. Selection of only certain parts of what an individual has done or said which causes a negative view of their action.

Hoaxes

Fabricated stories, mostly with sensational aspects, designed to trick the people into believing. A famous example is in Social Media where a celebrity is dead or has done something without any proof.

Rumors

Unverified information based on what people have said, this involves no verification, mostly dependent on hearsay.

Clickbait

Headlines or Content designed to lure people into clicking on a link by exaggeration and misinterpreting context.

Fake News

Completely Fabricated stories or headlines presented as news, often with sensational or manipulative goals.

Conspiracy Theories

Beliefs of events or situations with the result of assumptions of secret, often malicious plots by certain groups.

Misleading Information

Information that is only partially true in a way that gives a misleading impression.

Satire & Parody

Information and Stories that are exaggerated to ridiculous levels that it sounds unbelievable, however somehow it is still taken as fact by some people.

Out-of-Context Information

Taking the original information, statement, or image, and presenting without any context.

Pseudoscience

Information that is presented as scientific or factual but its not backed by research or data.

Manipulated Media

Images, videos, or audio that have been edited to mislead.

Case Studies

COVID-19 Microchip

Claim: Vaccines contain microchips for tracking.

Reality: Proven false by medical experts.

Types: Conspiracy Theory, Pseudoscience, Fake News.

Impact: Fear and vaccine hesitancy.

Typhoon Ulysses Aid Hoarding

Claim: Aid for families was being hoarded by political figures.

Reality: Photos were edited and unverified.

Types: Hoaxes, Manipulated Media.

Impact: Public outrage and mistrust.

Dengvaxia Vaccine Scare

Claim: Dengvaxia caused deaths among all children.

Reality: Risks were exaggerated; vaccine was safe for those previously infected.

Types: Misrepresentation, Fearmongering.

Impact: Vaccine hesitancy, measles and polio outbreaks.

2022 Elections Disinformation

Claim: Manipulated posts and fake quotes about candidates circulated widely.

Reality: Fact-checkers disproved many altered photos and narratives.

Types: Fake News, Manipulated Media.

Impact: Misled voters, fueled political division.
